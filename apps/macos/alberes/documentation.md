# Alberes Advanced Time Tracker Documentation

## Overview

Alberes Advanced Time Tracker is a lightweight native macOS menu bar application designed for systems-based productivity. It tracks life events down to 1-second intervals for absolute precision. It caters to a dedicated niche of productivity enthusiasts who require granular analytics and control over their time data.

Alberes records locally and respects your privacy. All your tracking history is kept on-device.

---

## Getting Started

### System Requirements
- macOS 14 or later

### Installation & Launch
For a normal download, open the `.dmg`, drag `AlberesAdvancedTimeTracker.app` to `Applications`, then open it from `Applications`. 

The app lives in your macOS menu bar. 

---

## Using Alberes

### The Menu Bar Interface
Alberes is designed to be unobtrusive. Access the app directly from your menu bar to start, stop, and switch tracking sessions instantly.
You can customize the menu bar display in Settings to show:
- Icon only
- Timer only
- Timer + Chapter name

### Chapters and Categorization
Alberes groups your time into "Chapters" (e.g., Deep Work, Admin, Learning). 
- You can create, edit, color-code, and delete custom Chapters.
- Assign **Billable Rates** to any Chapter to see estimated earnings in your analytics.
- Add text **Notes & Descriptions** to any active or historical session.

### Focus Mode (Pomodoro)
By default, the tracker counts up. You can toggle **Focus Mode** to count down (e.g., standard 25-minute Pomodoro) with optional break blocks. This automatically logs your focused time to the selected Chapter.

### Global Keyboard Shortcuts
Control tracking (start, stop, switch chapter) globally without touching the mouse, keeping your hands on the keyboard and your focus uninterrupted. *(Note: macOS may require Accessibility or Input Monitoring permissions).*

### Idle Detection
If you step away from your Mac, Alberes detects the idle time and prompts you when you return. You can choose to assign the idle time to a Chapter or discard it entirely.

## Analytics and Goals

### The Analytics Dashboard
View detailed visual analytics, including daily and weekly timelines, categorical breakdowns, and your overall productivity patterns.

### Weekly Goals
Set time budgets! For example, set a "Minimum 20 hours a week" on a Work chapter or a "Maximum 5 hours" on a Social media chapter. The Analytics tab will display your progress via visual rings.

### Background Reports
Alberes generates automated HTML reports summarizing your tracking history and delivers them straight to your email.

---

## Data Management & Export

### Manual Adjustments
The History tab lists all past entries. You can retroactively edit, split, or merge time blocks with 1-second precision if you forgot to stop a timer.

### Exporting Your Data
Your raw time entries live securely on your Mac. Export your entire history to **CSV** or **JSON** to analyze it in your own external tools, spreadsheets, or scripts. 

You can also generate PDF reports or invoices to send directly to clients if you use Alberes for freelance billing.

## Troubleshooting & Support

*   **The timer did not resume after relaunch:** Alberes resumes the last active session automatically. If it does not, quit and reopen the app once, then check the History tab.
*   **Global shortcuts are not working:** macOS requires Accessibility or Input Monitoring permission for background keyboard monitoring. Re-open the app after granting permission in System Settings.
*   **Idle time was assigned incorrectly:** Open the History tab and manually edit the affected entry.
*   **Export is disabled:** Check your active subscription or trial status in the Billing & Plans tab.
